# Worked Example — Adaptive Clothing (from workshop demo)

This is the Day 1 live path (elderly adaptive apparel), rewritten as a **hackathon pattern** you can copy.

## 0. Raw intent (multi-part — messy is OK)

> Building adaptive apparel for elderly with neurological conditions (and maybe neurodivergent kids). Markets: UK, Germany, France, India. Want: clothing struggles, makeshift solutions, whether our solution fits, how to communicate so they buy.

## 1. Brief Builder friction (what good looks like)

**Mirror:** four questions in one.  
**Audience split:** elderly neuro ≠ neurodivergent children (different buyers, territories, emotion).  
**Primary for v1:** elderly + caregivers (UK first).

### Living question (workshop spirit)
> Do people living with neurological conditions (and those who dress them) experience clothing struggle as a **named, solvable problem**, or have they **normalized** it into something they no longer reach for a product to fix — and if they reach, what must clothing **not announce**?

### Why this is “living”
Answer A → education/activation problem.  
Answer B → product + identity design problem.  
Changes GTM and MVP.

## 2. Territories (lived pain)

| Rank | Territory | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Reddit caregivers / aging parents / dementia-adjacent | Adult children hold pain; anonymous honesty |
| 2 | Facebook groups (browser/login) | Peer support mid-struggle |
| 3 | Amazon / product reviews | Money spent; workarounds on “normal” clothes |
| 4 | YouTube / TikTok | Visual dressing rituals, emotion |

**Absence to note:** first-person patient voice often missing online → caregivers as proxy.

## 3. Void language (example outcomes from the teaching)

| Phrase / pattern | Product implication |
|---|---|
| Clothes that **keep quiet about it** | Don’t announce illness |
| Adaptive looks like **you’ve given up** | Aesthetic identity > pure function |
| Morning battle treated as **dementia behavior** | Misclassification → design problem, not only patient “noncompliance” |
| Makeshift mods to normal clothes | MVP = remove the mod pain |

## 4. Insight → demo spine (hackathon translation)

**Demo spine:**  
Caregiver morning flow → “normal clothes fail mid-dress” → product that preserves dignity / doesn’t look clinical → relief moment.

**Cut list:** multi-country launch, kids line, full marketplace, complex sizing AI — all Day-2+ .

**Hero line pattern:**  
Use field language about dignity / not announcing / not looking like you’ve given up — not “AI-powered adaptive wearables.”

## 5. Evidence posture

- Pitch-grade: caregiver quotes + review workarounds + cross-place echo  
- CANDIDATE: any single hallucinated product category until validated  
- Optional later: survey sizing for stakeholders who need numbers

## 7. What to steal for ANY hackathon idea

1. Messy multi-part intent → one living question  
2. One audience first  
3. Proxy speakers when primary is silent  
4. Void language > feature list  
5. MVP = automate the makeshift  
6. Identity/aesthetic constraints often beat pure function  
7. Deploy full brief; bank surprises
