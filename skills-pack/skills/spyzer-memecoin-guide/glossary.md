# Glossary

**Accountability habit** - Reviewing misses and losses to identify a specific process failure and rule. (Ch 10)

**Attention arbitrage** - Trading the gap between a narrative gaining attention and the market fully repricing the right asset. (Ch 3)

**Bear market** - Conditions where majors trend down, on-chain volume dries up, groups get quiet, and fresh launches top lower. (Ch 8)

**Borrowed conviction** - Buying because another person is confident without understanding their thesis, entry, size, or exit. (Ch 5)

**Break of structure** - A chart violating the swing high or low that should have held if the trend remained intact. (Ch 7)

**Bundling** - One entity controlling token supply through multiple wallets. (Ch 2)

**CA / Contract Address** - Unique address used to identify a token or pool. (Ch 2, Ch 4)

**CEX** - Centralized exchange; convenient custody that can freeze or lose user access. (Ch 12)

**Cold wallet** - Offline or hardware wallet used to protect significant holdings. (Ch 12)

**Community coin** - Coin whose value depends on persistent community coordination, content, raids, and onboarding. (Ch 3)

**Confluence** - Multiple independent signals aligning around the same trade zone or thesis. (Ch 7)

**CTO** - Community takeover after the original developer or team abandons the coin. (Ch 3)

**DYOR** - Do your own research; verify before acting. (Ch 1, Ch 4)

**Exit liquidity** - Buyers who allow earlier holders to sell profitably. (Ch 5)

**Fibonacci retracement** - Technical tool using pullback levels such as 0.5, 0.618, and 0.786. (Ch 7)

**Fish** - Trader type focused on steady, lower-variance compounding. (Ch 10)

**FOMO entry** - Buying from urgency caused by price movement rather than thesis. (Ch 9)

**Fumble spiral** - Emotional deterioration after missing or mishandling a major winner. (Ch 10)

**Gas fee** - Cost paid to submit a transaction. (Ch 2)

**Honeypot** - Token setup that lures buys and prevents or punishes selling. (Ch 2, Ch 12)

**Hot wallet** - Internet-connected wallet used for active trading or dapp interaction. (Ch 12)

**Information deficiency edge** - Advantage from knowing relevant information before most market participants. (Ch 4, Ch 9)

**Invalidation** - Condition where the trade idea no longer makes sense. (Ch 7, Ch 9)

**KOL** - Key Opinion Leader; crypto influencer or public caller. (Ch 5)

**Liquidity Pool / LP** - Paired assets that enable token swaps and determine price impact. (Ch 2)

**Market cap / MC** - Price multiplied by token supply. (Ch 2)

**Market structure** - Pattern of highs and lows used to identify trend direction. (Ch 7)

**Mint authority** - Permission to create more tokens. (Ch 2)

**Monkey** - Trader type built for high-variance moonshot trades. (Ch 10)

**Multisig** - Wallet setup requiring multiple approvals to move funds. (Ch 12)

**NFA** - Not financial advice. (Ch 1)

**On-chain sleuthing** - Using explorers and wallet history to verify claims and behavior. (Ch 2, Ch 6)

**Ownership coin** - Token model tied conceptually to company valuation or equity-like debt exposure. (Ch 3)

**Paper trading** - Tracking hypothetical trades without risking real capital. (Ch 13)

**Phishing** - Fake site, link, or message designed to steal keys, signatures, or funds. (Ch 12)

**Position sizing** - Choosing how much capital a trade deserves. (Ch 9)

**Risk dial** - Adjusting aggressiveness and asset type based on market conditions. (Ch 8)

**Roundtripping** - Letting major unrealized profit return toward entry or loss. (Ch 1, Ch 9)

**Scarcity brain** - Fear-based mindset that treats every trade as rare and irreplaceable. (Ch 10)

**Seed phrase** - 12-24 word master recovery key for a wallet. (Ch 2, Ch 12)

**Slippage** - Price difference caused by execution impact or other trades moving before yours. (Ch 2)

**Telegram call channel** - Channel that posts coin calls or market leads. (Ch 4, Ch 5)

**Thesis** - The reason a trade should work, plus what would invalidate it. (Ch 4, Ch 9)

**Token account** - Solana account used to hold a specific token for a wallet. (Ch 6)

**Total Value Locked / TVL** - Value held in a pool or protocol. (Ch 2)

**Trenches** - Fast, chaotic fresh-launch memecoin trading environment. (Ch 8)

**Utility coin** - Token with product, access, revenue, burn, buyback, or usage mechanics. (Ch 3)

**Vamping** - A rival coin stealing attention and liquidity from the coin initially tied to a narrative. (Ch 3)

**Wallet tracker** - Tool or habit for monitoring another trader's wallet activity. (Ch 6)
