## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

**More informed tends to mean more profitable**: use every trade as an information problem before treating it as a price problem. Check narrative, supply, holders, social proof, chain data, market conditions, and your own psychology before sizing.

**Awareness is not learning**: knowing a rule does not mean you will obey it when money is moving. Convert painful mistakes into concrete rules, then rehearse those rules before the next similar moment.

**Attention drives value, but through different mechanisms**: memecoins run on raw attention; utility coins blend attention with product, revenue, buybacks, or burns; ownership coins point toward company valuation exposure. Match your time horizon and exit logic to the coin type.

**Attention arbitrage**: spot the gap between a narrative becoming viral and the market repricing the related coin. The edge is earliest when the public story is forming but crypto attention has not fully coordinated around the right asset.

**Canonical identity beats vague narrative**: a coin is less vulnerable to copies when the name, creator, launch surface, community, and discovery platforms all point at one obvious leader.

**Vamping risk**: when multiple coins compete for the same narrative, liquidity rotates to the version the market accepts as canonical. Avoid buying vague placeholder coins when the true name, source, or leader is still unresolved.

**Bundling is supply control, not automatically good or bad**: a bundled supply can stabilize a serious team or let insiders farm buyers. Judge intent and risk through cluster links, top-holder percentage, funding origin, chart shape, volume-to-market-cap, and wallet behavior.

**Do not borrow conviction**: KOLs, Telegram calls, fomo notifications, and wallet trackers should surface information, not make decisions. If you cannot explain why you are buying in two sentences, you are not ready to buy.

**Information streams compound**: build a private group, a filtered X feed, app/social notes, wallet monitoring, and on-chain sleuthing. A small trusted group sharing real theses beats a giant public channel.

**Thesis before entry**: write why you are buying, what would invalidate the thesis, and what would make you take profit before entering. A trade changes as new information arrives; keep asking whether you would buy the same size now.

**Market structure first**: ask whether price is going up, down, sideways, or transitioning. Higher highs and higher lows mean uptrend; lower highs and lower lows mean downtrend. A break of structure is one of the cleanest signals that conditions changed.

**Late confirmation beats early hope**: buying after a confirmed uptrend is often better than trying to catch a bottom in a downtrend. Being early is only useful when your information edge is real.

**Confluence over isolated signals**: a Fibonacci zone matters more when it aligns with prior support/resistance, trendlines, round numbers, volume, or narrative strength. One signal is possible; multiple signals become tradable.

**The risk dial**: in strong bull conditions, more risk can flow toward memecoins and fresh attention narratives. As conditions worsen, rotate toward utility, ownership, stable assets, or cash. Do less when the market gives less.

**Sizing should preserve the next decision**: size so a win matters and a loss does not damage your ability to trade normally tomorrow. Oversizing creates emotional debt; emotional debt creates revenge trades.

**Spreading kills returns**: too many small positions dilute winners and make follow-up impossible. Prefer concentrated conviction in the best-researched ideas you can actually monitor.

**Take profits gradually**: scale out as price moves in your favor instead of trying to time the top. If the money is life-changing, secure it now. A screen number is not freedom until it is protected.

**You are not trying to make the money back; you are trying to make money**: after a loss or fumble, identify the process failure, create a rule, detach from the old equity high, and trade the next setup cleanly.

**Fish & Monkey Methodology**: know your natural trader type. Fish prefer consistent compounding and smaller risk; monkeys prefer high-variance moonshots. Do not force yourself into a temperament that destroys your process.

**Scarcity brain**: financial pressure makes every trade feel like the only chance. Build a safety floor, trade from conviction instead of survival panic, and remember that opportunities are abundant.

**Only the paranoid survive**: crypto security is a habit. Use separate wallets, protect seed phrases offline, verify URLs, distrust unsolicited DMs, keep long-term holdings in cold storage, and never expose your full portfolio socially.
