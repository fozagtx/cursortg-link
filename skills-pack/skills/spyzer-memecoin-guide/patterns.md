# Patterns

## Pre-Trade Thesis Gate
**When to use**: Before entering any meaningful trade.
**How**: Write: why this coin, why now, what information is underpriced, what invalidates the trade, how much you will risk, and when you will take profit.
**Trade-offs**: Slower than impulse buying, but it prevents fog-brain entries and improves post-trade review.

## Fresh Coin Safety Scan
**When to use**: Before buying low-cap or newly launched tokens.
**How**: Check LP lock, mint/freeze authority, holder concentration, bundling signs, funding origin, volume-to-market-cap, and chart shape.
**Trade-offs**: May make you miss some fast trades, but avoids the fastest paths to zero.

## Bundling Detection Stack
**When to use**: When supply distribution looks suspicious or chart action is too clean.
**How**: Combine Bubblemaps clusters, top-holder percentages, fresh wallet labels, bundler icons, funding origin, bot-like candles, MC vs volume, and fees paid.
**Trade-offs**: No single signal proves malicious intent; use the stack to price risk.

## Narrative Canonicality Check
**When to use**: During viral animal, celebrity, news, or phrase trades.
**How**: Identify the exact name/source, official or dominant coin, chain, launchpad, and community. Avoid vague placeholder coins if better identity evidence is pending.
**Trade-offs**: Waiting for clarity may cost early entry, but reduces vamping risk.

## X Contract Search
**When to use**: When researching a coin's social narrative.
**How**: Search both CA and ticker in Top and Latest. Skip bot/call-channel spam. Look for human theses, credible early posters, communities, dev accounts, and contradictory evidence.
**Trade-offs**: Noisy, but often reveals why price is moving.

## Dev/Project Legitimacy Trace
**When to use**: For utility, team, or product-linked tokens.
**How**: Verify account history, mutuals, LinkedIn, Discord, old posts, prior projects, hacked-account risk, and confirmation from trusted sources.
**Trade-offs**: Deep research takes time; the edge decays as facts become public.

## KOL Trust Audit
**When to use**: Before using an influencer as a source.
**How**: Track calls over time. Note deleted posts, honesty about losses, update quality, paid-promo patterns, and whether they post reasoning or only results.
**Trade-offs**: Requires patience; avoids mistaking audience for skill.

## Wallet Discovery via Explorer
**When to use**: To verify or monitor a trader/holder.
**How**: Find the token chain, copy CA, open the explorer, inspect holders, match token balance to app display, and confirm with recent transactions.
**Trade-offs**: Wallets may be partial or decoys; do not copy-trade blindly.

## Market Structure Read
**When to use**: Before technical entries or exits.
**How**: Identify higher highs/lows or lower highs/lows. Look for break of structure to confirm trend change.
**Trade-offs**: Simple but not perfect; combine with thesis and market conditions.

## Fib Confluence Entry
**When to use**: After a clean directional impulse.
**How**: Draw body-to-body in memecoins from swing low to high. Watch 0.5, 0.618, and 0.786 zones. Prefer entries where fib aligns with support/resistance, trendlines, round numbers, or high-volume areas.
**Trade-offs**: Invalid in chop or unfinished moves.

## Risk Dial Adjustment
**When to use**: Weekly, daily, or whenever conditions shift.
**How**: Assess majors, on-chain volume, fresh-launch ceilings, group activity, and outside attention. Increase risk in strong conditions; reduce size or stop trading in weak ones.
**Trade-offs**: Can feel boring in cold markets; preserves capital for real opportunity.

## Current-Size Test
**When to use**: After a position moves or the thesis changes.
**How**: Ask: if I found this coin today at this price, how much would I buy? Sell the difference between current holdings and current justified size.
**Trade-offs**: May exit before further upside; prevents stale thesis exposure.

## Profit Scaling
**When to use**: On large unrealized gains.
**How**: Sell portions into strength, especially when euphoria appears or the gain changes your life. Keep a runner only if the thesis still supports it.
**Trade-offs**: You will never sell the exact top; that is the point.

## Loss-to-Rule Review
**When to use**: After a significant loss or missed trade.
**How**: Identify the precise failure and write one concrete rule. Examples: max size under low MC, reduce on uncertain bearish news, no entry without thesis, no trading after major fumble.
**Trade-offs**: Emotionally uncomfortable; accelerates real learning.

## Wallet Blast-Radius Design
**When to use**: Before storing or trading meaningful amounts.
**How**: Separate active trading wallet, browsing/dapp wallet, long-term cold wallet, and optionally multisig/extra cold storage for very large holdings.
**Trade-offs**: More operational complexity; sharply reduces single-failure risk.
