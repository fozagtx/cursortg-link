# Chapter 13: Your First Steps

## Core Idea
Beginners should set up infrastructure, safety, information streams, and practice habits before risking meaningful money. The order matters because early mistakes compound quickly in crypto.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Setup before speculation**: do not fund or trade before wallets and backups are ready.
  - When to use: when onboarding from zero.
  - How: set up trading app, export keys, create separate wallet, back up seed phrase, then fund small.
- **Feed-building as training**: a good X feed teaches narratives and pattern recognition.
  - When to use: before relying on calls.
  - How: follow gradually, prioritize thoughtful researchers, post your own thoughts, and avoid wins-only shillers.
- **Private group first**: build or join a small trust network before chasing public channels.
  - When to use: after creating Telegram.
  - How: add a scanner bot, invite genuine traders over time, and use the group to share theses and checks.
- **Observe before risking**: paper trading teaches process without tuition.
  - When to use: before first real memecoin trades.
  - How: write hypothetical entry, thesis, invalidation, and management; then follow the outcome.
- **First withdrawal rule**: make early wins real.
  - When to use: after first meaningful profit.
  - How: withdraw some to a bank or protected storage to feel and protect the win.

## Key Concepts
- **Trading wallet**: wallet used for active app execution.
- **Navigation wallet**: separate wallet used for dapps and exploration.
- **USDC/cash balance**: stable value used to deploy across opportunities.
- **X feed**: narrative discovery stream.
- **Telegram group**: real-time research and coordination channel.
- **Paper trading**: simulated trades tracked without risking money.

## Mental Models
- Use **first deposits as tuition**, not income.
- Think **20 follows at a time** when building a feed; quality and account safety matter.
- Treat **posting as thesis practice** even with no followers.
- Use **withdrawal as psychological anchoring**: it proves profit is real and worth protecting.

## Anti-patterns
- **Skipping key backups**: funding wallets before exporting or storing recovery material.
- **Following everyone at once**: noisy feeds and platform limits reduce quality.
- **Starting with public call channels**: beginners often lack timing and exit skill.
- **Paper trading casually**: it only works if entries, exits, and reasons are written.
- **Compounding every win immediately**: never feeling real profit makes it easier to lose it all.

## Worked Example
From zero: install the trading app, export and store keys, create a separate Phantom wallet with seed backups, fund only an amount you can lose, build an X feed gradually, create a Telegram group with a bot, paper trade until thesis writing feels natural, then take small real trades. On the first meaningful win, withdraw some profit.

## Key Takeaways
1. Do not reorder setup, safety, funding, feed, group, observation, and trading.
2. Start small enough that losses teach rather than harm.
3. Build original thought by posting and paper trading.
4. Use public channels as later supplements, not beginner foundations.
5. Protect the first win so the process becomes real.
