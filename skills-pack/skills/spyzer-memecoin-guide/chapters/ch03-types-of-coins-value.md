# Chapter 3: Types of Coins & What Drives Value

## Core Idea
Coins rise for different reasons. The common thread is attention and value accrual, but memecoins, utility coins, and ownership coins require different research, time horizons, and exits.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Attention arbitrage**: profit from the lag between real-world or crypto-native attention and market repricing.
  - When to use: when a viral animal, phrase, person, event, ecosystem launch, or cultural meme starts spreading.
  - How: identify the canonical subject, verify the right coin, check supply risk, and act before attention fully coordinates.
- **Vamping risk**: a competing coin can steal the narrative if it becomes the recognized leader.
  - When to use: when the token name, ticker, chain, launchpad, or source identity is ambiguous.
  - How: wait for or find the canonical name/source, then compare liquidity and social coordination.
- **Coin-type fit**: match your trade logic to the asset category.
  - When to use: before deciding how long to hold.
  - How: ask whether the coin is raw meme attention, community conviction, utility/product value, or company valuation exposure.
- **Community conviction flywheel**: active communities can sustain attention beyond a one-off viral event.
  - When to use: when evaluating longer-running memes.
  - How: inspect whether members keep creating, raiding, onboarding, and posting even when price is down.

## Key Concepts
- **Viral memecoin**: coin tied to a fast-spreading external event or figure.
- **Celebrity memecoin**: token tied to a public figure's launch or presence.
- **CTO**: community takeover after original dev/team leaves.
- **Community coin**: coin whose durability comes from persistent social coordination.
- **Utility coin**: token with product, fees, burns, buybacks, access, or revenue mechanics.
- **Ownership coin**: tokenized exposure conceptually tied to company valuation or debt-like equity exposure.
- **Vamping**: liquidity and attention rotating from one narrative coin to a rival.
- **Canonical identity**: the coin the market accepts as the real representative of the narrative.

## Mental Models
- Use **attention first, mechanics second** for pure memecoins.
- Use **one-sentence undervaluation** for attention-driven utility trades: the easier the story is to explain, the faster lazy liquidity can move.
- Think of **pure memecoins as churn by design** unless they gain identity, distribution, or product gravity.
- Treat **community as compounding labor**: many small daily replies, posts, and DMs can create durable mindshare.

## Anti-patterns
- **Applying one category's logic to another**: memecoin holding logic can exit ownership coins too early; ownership logic can hold memecoins too long.
- **Buying a placeholder name**: vague tickers get vamped when the true name is found.
- **Confusing seriousness with value**: serious crypto developments can create better memecoin attention than whitepaper analysis.
- **Joining inactive communities**: a group that only posts when price rises is not durable.

## Worked Example
A viral animal appears online, but the first token uses a generic placeholder name. Research reveals the animal's actual name from the source site. A second token with the real name launches at a lower market cap and starts attracting people who discover the true identity. The edge is not just being early to virality; it is being early to the canonical version before rotation.

## Key Takeaways
1. Attention is the raw fuel, but each coin type converts it differently.
2. Vamping is structural because launchpads, creators, and traders all benefit from new alternatives.
3. Community coins can outlast event coins when the meme is timeless and the group works.
4. Utility trades can still be attention trades when the narrative is simple and obvious.
5. Before trading, classify the coin; then choose the correct research checklist and exit logic.
