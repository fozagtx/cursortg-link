# Chapter 7: Reading Charts

## Core Idea
Chart reading starts with one question: is price going up, going down, going sideways, or transitioning? Market structure is the foundation; indicators and drawings only help when they clarify that answer.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Market structure**: trends are made of higher highs/lows or lower highs/lows.
  - When to use: before buying or selling based on price action.
  - How: identify recent swing highs/lows and whether the pattern has broken.
- **Timeframe fit**: chart timeframe must match the asset and holding period.
  - When to use: before drawing levels.
  - How: use high timeframes for long holds, very low timeframes for fresh launches, and more meaningful levels only after enough price history exists.
- **Fibonacci retracement zone**: use 0.5 to 0.786 as a pullback entry zone in directional moves.
  - When to use: after a clean impulse has formed.
  - How: draw from higher low to higher high in an uptrend; use body-to-body on low-liquidity memecoins.
- **Confluence and invalidation**: levels matter more when multiple signals align and the setup has a defined failure point.
  - When to use: before entering or holding a technical setup.
  - How: combine fibs with support/resistance, trendlines, round numbers, volume, and thesis; exit or reduce when structure breaks.

## Key Concepts
- **Higher high / higher low**: uptrend structure.
- **Lower high / lower low**: downtrend structure.
- **Break of structure**: price violates the swing point that should have held.
- **Retracement**: pullback within a broader trend.
- **0.618**: key Fibonacci level often called the golden zone.
- **Confluence**: multiple independent signals pointing to the same zone.
- **Invalidation**: the point where the trade idea no longer makes sense.
- **Wick vs body**: extreme price tail versus open/close region of a candle.

## Mental Models
- Use **late confirmation over early hopium**: a confirmed uptrend is safer than guessing a bottom.
- Treat **charts as one information stream**, not the whole thesis.
- Think **body-to-body in memecoins** because single buyers can create misleading wicks.
- Use **zoom out when panicking**: higher timeframes separate noise from structural change.

## Anti-patterns
- **Drawing on newborn charts**: fresh launches do not yet have meaningful levels.
- **Catching bottoms**: buying downtrends without strength is hope, not structure.
- **Using fibs inside ranges**: no impulse means no meaningful retracement.
- **Refusing invalidation**: a setup that breaks must be re-evaluated, not defended.

## Worked Example
A coin prints a clean high, pulls back, and the 0.618 retracement aligns with a prior all-time-high support zone. That is stronger than a naked fib level. If price later closes below the deeper retracement and retests it from underneath as resistance, the setup is likely dead and should be cut or reduced.

## Key Takeaways
1. Uptrend, downtrend, sideways, transition: answer this first.
2. Market structure is more important than indicators.
3. You do not need to buy the exact bottom to make serious money.
4. Fibonacci helps only after a clean move exists.
5. Thesis first, chart for entry and challenge.
